Per

A minimal cubical CCHM implementation of MLTT-80, Martin-L├╢f Intuitionistic Type Theory. Per is an internalized model capable of checking theorems about itself, providing a foundational core for higher-order dependently typed systems.

Abstract

Per is a theorem prover that constitutes a MLTT-80 core for a dependently-typed lambda calculus. It is specifically constrained to exclude pattern matching, let-bindings, and implicit arguments to focus on the pure mathematical properties of the core. It encompasses two countable hierarchies of universes U and V for fibrational Kan types and Interval pretypes as in HTS, dependent products Pi, dependent pairs Sigma, identity types Id, and 0, 1, 2, W types for well-founded definitions.

Unlike full CCHM cubical HIT-enriched systems with complex composition structures, Per remains a minimal cubical system with Glue, Partial, Subtype in addition to hcomp and transp operations. It focuses on correctness, soundness, totality, and canonicity тАФ the essential attributes for formal mathematics and self-internalized type theory.

Self-Checking Core

Internalized Model

Per is designed to be self-hosting in its logic. The core constructs are represented within the language itself, allowing for proofs about the typechecker's own behaviors and properties.

Foundational Focus

Per excludes high-level sugar to maintain a lean, provable core. It leverages Judgmental Equality and Strong Normalization, ensuring that every well-typed term reduces to a unique normal form, even in the presence of universes and well-founded types.

DNF Solver for Homotopy Cubes

The CCHM (Cubical Computational Higher Type Theory) model requires a robust solver for face lattice operations. Per implements a simple DNF (Disjunctive Normal Form) solver to handle boundary conditions in hcomp and transp operations. This solver is essential for ensuring that composition across different faces remains consistent and that judgmental equality between complex face expressions is decidable.
